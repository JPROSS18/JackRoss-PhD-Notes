
Distances between functions: 

$$
\begin{aligned}
& C^0 = \sup_{x \in J} | f(x) - g(x) | \\
& C^r = \sup_{x \in J} | f(x) - g(x) | + |f'(x) - g'(x)| + .... +|f^{(r)}(x) - g^{(r)}|  
\end{aligned}
$$

Definition: 

Let $f: X \to X$. $f$ is $C^r$ is *structurally stable* on J if $\exists \varepsilon > 0$ s.t whenever $d_r(f, g) < \varepsilon$ for $g: X \to X$. It follows that $f$ is topologically conjugate to $g$.   

**Example:**
The logistic map is $C^2$ structurally stable for $\forall \mu > 2 +\sqrt5$.
